How to Use This Workbook

This workbook is your personal companion for building six powerful dream duas — supplications so specific, vivid, and connected to your deepest aspirations that they give you butterflies every time you make them.

It is based on the Visionaire methodology by Shaykh Muhammad al-Shareef, adapted and personalized by UmmAbdullah for her own spiritual journey.

What makes a dream dua?
  • It is slightly unrealistic — it gives you butterflies
  • It is concrete, not vague ("60kg, strong knees" not just "good health")
  • It spans a 3–6 year horizon
  • It terrifies AND amazes you at the same time
  • It is connected to your Akhira intentions

How to Work Through Each Section

  1. Read the foundation teaching and reflect on it
  2. Write in the spaces provided — these are for you alone, between you and Allah
  3. Revisit and refine your dream duas regularly — they will grow as you grow
  4. Recite them daily, especially in the last third of the night
"And your Lord says: Call upon Me; I will respond to you." 📖 Surah Ghafir (40:60)

Begin with bismillah. Trust that He is listening. And remember — your dua is not too big. Your dua is smaller than His generosity.

Part One: The Foundation of Dua

How Dua Works — The Story That Started It All

Shaykh Muhammad al-Shareef was locked out in Mecca. He climbed the balcony — still couldn't get in. In complete helplessness, he remembered this verse:

"Is He [not best] who responds to the desperate one when he calls upon Him and removes evil and makes you inheritors of the earth? Is there a deity with Allah? Little do you remember." 📖 Surah An-Naml (27:62)

He made dua with full trust in Allah — and the door opened. That experience launched his lifelong study of dua. Earlier, at age 11, during the last ten nights, he had made dua to become a hafiz. He did.

Key Insight: Sometimes you work toward the answer. Other times there is no way you can control it — Allah just makes it happen. Both are from Him. Both require dua.

What does this story make you feel? What does it remind you of in your own life?

We need both. Don't only call on Allah when desperate — call on Him when you're safe, hopeful, and dreaming too.

Reflect: Are your duas mostly reactive (in hardship) or proactive (in hope)? What would change if you made proactive duas daily?

Part Two: Core Principles

Your Dua Is Higher Than You Think

Your dua doesn't match where you are right now — it is higher than you are. And that is the point. The dua changes YOU. Every time you make it, you evolve a little more toward the person worthy of that answer. The dua is your destination; you are the one walking toward it.

Which of your duas feels "too big"? Write it here — unfiltered:

Visualize Before You Ask

Shaykh Muhammad al-Shareef and his wife did not have children for 10 years. He noticed his dua had become habitual — words without heart. Then he changed: he started imagining. His daughter grown. Her wedding day. Him brushing her hair. His sons, leading salah. He saw it in vivid detail — then he made dua. And it was answered.

The Technique: Before you make your dua, close your eyes. Picture the answered version. What do you see? Who is with you? What does it feel like? What time of day is it? Hold that image — then open your hands and ask.

Practice now: Choose one dream dua. Describe in writing exactly what it looks like when it is answered:

Part Three: My Six Dream Duas

For each life area, you will find: what to be grateful for, what you sense is lacking, a sample dream dua, and generous space to craft your own.

"So which of the favors of your Lord would you deny?" 📖 Surah Ar-Rahman (55:13) — repeated 31 times
1 Health

Mentors: Seek inspiration from those who honored their bodies as amanah — the Prophet ﷺ, Aisha (RA) who lived actively, and Maryam (AS) who was sustained by Allah alone when she had nothing.

Gratitude Inventory

Allah's ability to heal, strength, recovery from illness, no major dependency. Pregnancy at 43 despite hardship — the child who waited in Jannah. A functioning body that wakes each morning and returns to Allah in sajdah.

What are YOU specifically grateful for in your health today?

Honest Reflection

What habits or patterns are working against your health? Be honest with yourself:

Sample Dream Dua

Dream Dua — Health: Ya Allah, I am an amanah from You. Grant me the strength and wisdom to care for myself. Make me reach optimal health — 60kg, glowing skin, strong body, no pain in my knees or back. Give me energy to run, swim, and play with my children without exhaustion. Grant me the strength to stand long in qiyam al-layl without fatigue, to fast with ease. Make my body a tool for 'ibadah, not an obstacle to it. Ya Allah, grant me the highest emotional intelligence — firm yet kind, unaffected by negativity, navigating difficult people with grace. Give me wisdom and sound judgment. Let me make good decisions confidently and stick to them without second-guessing myself. Free me from the prison of overthinking. When I make mistakes, let me learn and move forward — never hating myself. Remind me You are Al-Ghaffar; if You forgive me, I must forgive myself. Let me care for this body and mind so I can serve You with full capacity until my last breath.

Now write YOUR health dream dua. Make it vivid, specific, and connected to your 'ibadah:

How does your dream health enable your worship and purpose?

2 Wealth

Mentors: Anas ibn Malik, Abdurrahman ibn Awf, Uthman ibn Affan — companions who had wealth in their hands, not their hearts, and used it to build the ummah.

Gratitude Inventory

Own home, car, no payment stress, successful business, multiple income sources. The ability to provide. Barakah in what you have.

What are you genuinely grateful for in your financial life?

Honest Reflection

What areas of wealth feel lacking — not from ingratitude but from aspiration to do more good?

Sample Dream Dua

Dream Dua — Wealth: Ya Allah, grant me wealth in my hand, not my heart. Let me spend so freely that I need accountants to track it — my right hand giving while my left doesn't know. Give me properties that pay themselves off, businesses that earn without constant effort. Grant me the barakah of Anas ibn Malik and the generosity of Uthman ibn Affan. Let me build a masjid where my sons lead as imams — let me sit in the front row watching them, tears streaming with gratitude. Make me run impactful charity projects — funding orphan education, building wells. $1M/year, growing to $10M. Financial freedom to focus on teaching Quran, taking my family on annual Umrah without ever thinking about cost. Ya Allah, make my wealth a bridge to Jannah, not a barrier.

Write YOUR wealth dream dua. Connect the money to impact — what good would you do with it?

What is the Akhira vision behind your wealth dua?

3 Deen

Mentors: Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, Ibrahim (AS), Khadija (RA) — people whose deen was their identity, their compass, and their greatest achievement.

Gratitude Inventory

Freedom to practice. Abundant access to Islamic knowledge — Quran memorization, tajweed, online courses, living scholars, books. A Muslim family and home.

What specific blessings in your deen practice are you grateful for today?

Honest Reflection

Where do you sense a gap between who you are spiritually and who you want to be?

Sample Dream Dua

Dream Dua — Deen: Ya Allah, make me someone who never misses tahajjud — who stands before You every night, longing for Your Face, tears falling in sincere conversation. Grant me khushu' so deep that my salah becomes the coolness of my eyes. Let me author Islamic resources that reach millions — books, apps, Hifz programs that attach hearts to Your Quran. Make my home a place where Quran is recited daily, where my children memorize at my feet during family halaqa. Grant me the knowledge of the scholars and the humility of the students. Let everyone around me feel peace in my presence. Put barakah in everything I say and do. Ya Allah, let me die with "La ilaha illAllah" on my tongue, having lived a life that pleased You.

Write YOUR deen dream dua. What does your spiritual best look like?

4 Spouse

Mentors: Khadija (RA) — supported her husband's mission without hesitation. Umm Sulaim (RA) — protected her husband's emotions before her own. Fatima (RA) — turned her hardships into tasbeeh.

Gratitude Inventory

Relaxed nature. Not demanding. Helps with the children. A home where peace is possible.

What do you genuinely appreciate about your spouse that you may not say aloud often enough?

Honest Reflection

What do you wish were different — not from fault, but from the dream you carry?

Sample Dream Dua

Dream Dua — Spouse: Ya Allah, make him complement and complete me. Let his akhlaaq inspire me to be better. Like Khadija, let me support his dreams that benefit the ummah. Like Umm Sulaim, let me shield him from emotional harm. Like Fatima, let me turn to You instead of complaining. Grant us love, trust, and deep affection. Make us excited to be together — going on dates, traveling, laughing like we just met. Let him treat me like the Prophet ﷺ treated Ayesha, and let me treat him like Khadija treated the Prophet ﷺ. Heal the wounds in his heart. Replace any harshness with gentleness, any sadness with sakina. Let us walk hand in hand through Madinah one day, old and gray, still in love. Ya Allah — reunite us in Jannatul Firdaus, together forever, our love perfected.

Write YOUR marriage dream dua. What does a beautiful marriage look like for you?

How would your marriage look in Jannah — what is your Akhira vision for your spouse?

5 Children

Mentors: Khadija, Asiya, Maryam (AS) — women who raised souls connected to Allah under every kind of circumstance.

Gratitude Inventory

Healthy children who still obey, confide in you, and choose to be with you. The gift of being their mother. Their fitrah — their natural inclination toward good.

Write something you love about each of your children that you are grateful for today:

Sample Dream Dua

Dream Dua — Children: Ya Allah, place the Quran in their hearts. Make them huffadh with excellent akhlaaq — humble, kind, wise, strong, confident. Let them be like the brothers of Yusuf (AS) in their bond, without envy. Make them leaders in the community, running successful businesses and impactful Islamic projects. Grant them righteous spouses and children even better than them. Let them seek Your pleasure without me asking. Protect them from harm, the evil eye, and fitnah. Surround them with righteous companions. Ya Allah, let me live to see them lead Eid prayer. Let me sit in the front row of their weddings, tears streaming, heart overflowing. Make them the coolness of my eyes in this dunya and my companions in Jannatul Firdaus.

Write YOUR children dream dua. What do you see for each child specifically?

Visualize: You are old. You are watching your children. What do you see?

6 Fun & Joy

Mentors: The Prophet ﷺ raced with Aisha. He played with his grandchildren. He smiled. Joy is sunnah. Rest is sunnah. Fun that draws you closer to Allah and your family is an act of worship.

Gratitude Inventory

Ability to eat out, enjoy time together, safe family and community, a home to rest in.

What brings you genuine joy? What makes you laugh? What restores you?

Sample Dream Dua

Dream Dua — Fun & Joy: Ya Allah, let us enjoy the tayyibat You've given us without guilt — good clothes, comfortable beds, a home that feels like a peaceful retreat. Grant us family Umrah every year, standing together at the Ka'bah, hearts united in worship. Let us vacation in places that take our breath away — watching Your creation in awe, drawing closer to You and each other. Give us beach holidays, mountain getaways, and adventures that become cherished memories. Let me have spa days knowing self-care is worship. Fill our weekends with board games, laughter, inside jokes, and no screens. Make our home a place we never want to leave — decorated beautifully, filled with warmth and light. Let rest feel sweet, not restless. Ya Allah, let our joy be halal, our fun be blessed, and our laughter echo with gratitude for You.

Write YOUR joy and fun dream dua. Give yourself permission to ask for happiness:
