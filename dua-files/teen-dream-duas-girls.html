You Are Not Just a Body

We live in a world that constantly tells you that your worth is in how you look—your weight, your skin, your hair, your outfit. Social media makes it worse. Every scroll is a comparison. And it is exhausting.

Here's what Allah says about you:

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Allah does not look at your appearance or your wealth, but He looks at your hearts and your deeds."

Sahih Muslim 2564

The world will never stop judging your outside. But Allah judges your inside. So while the world is obsessing over your body, you can be quietly building something far more powerful—a heart that knows Allah, a character that doesn't crack, and a vision for your life that no one can take from you.

True inner beauty = A heart connected to Allah. That's what Khadija had. That's what Maryam had. That's what Aisha had. And it made them the most extraordinary women who ever lived.

On Hijab & Modesty

Whether you wear hijab already or you're working toward it, this is for you.

وَقُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنَاتِ يَغْضُضْنَ مِنْ أَبْصَارِهِنَّ وَيَحْفَظْنَ فُرُوجَهُنَّ وَلَا يُبْدِينَ زِينَتَهُنَّ إِلَّا مَا ظَهَرَ مِنْهَا وَلْيَضْرِبْنَ بِخُمُرِهِنَّ عَلَىٰ جُيُوبِهِنَّ

"And tell the believing women to lower their gaze and guard their chastity, and not to reveal their adornments except what normally appears. Let them draw their veils over their chests..."

Surah An-Nur (24:31)

يَا أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ قُل لِّأَزْوَاجِكَ وَبَنَاتِكَ وَنِسَاءِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ يُدْنِينَ عَلَيْهِنَّ مِن جَلَابِيبِهِنَّ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ أَدْنَىٰ أَن يُعْرَفْنَ فَلَا يُؤْذَيْنَ

"O Prophet! Ask your wives, daughters, and believing women to draw their veils over their bodies. In this way it is more likely they will be recognized and not harassed."

Surah Al-Ahzab (33:59)

💎 What Hijab Really Is

Hijab is not a cage. It is a crown. It says: I am more than what you see. My value is not in my hair or my body. I answer to Allah, not to you.

The world tells you to show everything to be valued. Allah tells you that your modesty IS your value. That is an act of liberation, not oppression.

Important: Hijab is first in the heart—in your intention, your character, your speech, your gaze. The outer hijab reflects an inner commitment to Allah. Ask Allah to make you love it, not just wear it.

Beautiful Inside AND Outside

Taking care of your appearance is not un-Islamic. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Allah is Beautiful and loves beauty." (Muslim 91). You can be beautiful AND modest. You can take care of yourself AND not be defined by it.

Outside Beauty

Taking care of your health, your skin, your fitness, your presentation—all as a form of gratitude to Allah for this body He gave you as an amanah.

Inside Beauty

Your akhlaaq, your iman, your patience, your honesty, your kindness—the things that make people love you for DECADES, not just for a photo.

The trap: Spending 2 hours on your appearance and 2 minutes on your salah. Or doing the opposite—neglecting yourself and calling it piety. The goal is BOTH. Inside and outside. For Allah, not for Instagram.

Stories That Will Change How You Make Dua

Story 1: The Girl Who Couldn't Afford the Course

A girl desperately wanted to take an Islamic course but had absolutely no money. Her mother made dua for her—specifically, sincerely, with full belief. Days later, someone knocked on their door and returned money they had borrowed years ago—money the mother had completely given up on ever seeing again. It arrived at exactly the right time. The daughter took the course. The mother's dua was answered from a direction neither of them imagined.

💡 Allah provides from where you least expect. Make dua. Ask your mom to make dua. Then watch.

وَيَرْزُقْهُ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا يَحْتَسِبُ

"And He provides for him from where he does not expect."

Surah At-Talaq (65:3)
Story 2: The Student Who Cried at Night

Imagine a student with a major exam coming up. She gets up in the night, prays, and cries—genuinely cries—because she cares so much. That's sincere dua. She didn't force the tears. She cried because the stakes were real to her.

Now imagine making duas for your future—your career, your deen, your marriage—with that same level of care. That's what a dream dua does. When you make it vivid and specific enough, you will naturally cry. You'll pray with urgency. Your salah will become alive.

💡 The goal of dream duas is to make you care so much about your life that your salah transforms. You stop going through motions and start actually talking to Allah.

Story 3: Bilal's Mistake

Bilal made a dua. Someone told him his dua wasn't good enough—that he should change it to something more impressive, more "worthy."

That advice was wrong.

Your dua isn't lower than you think. YOUR DUA IS HIGHER THAN YOU. The dua doesn't change to match where you are right now—YOU change to rise to meet it. Every time you make your dream dua, you evolve a little more toward the person who deserves that answer. The dua is the destination. You are the one doing the walking.

💡 Don't shrink your duas to match your current self. Make them bigger, then let them pull you upward.

Story 4: The Man at Hajj Who Had Everything

A man was at Hajj on Mount Arafah—the greatest place to make dua on the greatest day of the year. His wife urged him to go up and make dua. He shrugged: "I have everything I need. You go."

This is a trap called contentment without aspiration. Being satisfied with your blessings is beautiful. But it doesn't mean you stop asking Allah for more. He is not a last resort. He is your first call. Rich or poor, comfortable or struggling—you always have something to ask for. Your deen. Your children. The ummah. Your Akhira. Jannah itself.

💡 Never say "I have enough, I don't need to ask." There is always something worthy of dua.

Story 5: The Plane That Was Going to Crash

Shaykh Muhammad al-Shareef was on a flight. Something went terribly wrong. Passengers were reciting shahada. People were preparing to meet Allah. His wife, in those terrifying final moments, made dua—not for safety, not for survival. Out of deep habit and sincere longing, she made dua for a child. Even while facing death, what her heart most wanted came out in dua.

The plane landed safely. And she got pregnant.

The Shaykh said: doctors had previously told them it was not possible. But he had learned his lesson: you make dua even when you've been told it's impossible. You make dua even when the doors seem shut. You make dua even on the plane that's going down.

💡 Never stop asking just because someone—even a doctor, even logic—told you it can't happen. Allah is not bound by human limits.

How Dream Duas Work

🧠 Your Dua Changes YOU

When you make a sincere dua repeatedly, you evolve into the person who deserves that answer. The dua is not just a request—it's a transformation process.

⚡ Level Up

Think of it like leveling up your character. Every sincere dua is XP invested in your future self—the scholar, the leader, the strong confident Muslim woman you're becoming.

🎯 Be Specific

"Make me good" is not a dua. "Make me memorize 2 juz by Ramadan and let me feel the Quran in my heart" — THAT is a dua.

🦋 Butterflies Test

If your dua doesn't excite you, it's not from your heart yet. Keep refining until it gives you butterflies. That's how you know it's real.

وَمَا تَشَاءُونَ إِلَّا أَن يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ رَبُّ الْعَالَمِينَ

"And you do not will except that Allah wills—Lord of all the worlds."

Surah At-Takwir (81:29)

You are not asking a genie. You are asking the Owner of Everything. Nothing is too big.

Visualize First, Then Ask

Before making your dua, close your eyes and SEE it. Don't just say the words—feel them.

  • See yourself in that university, confident and covered
  • See yourself leading an Islamic project that changes lives
  • See yourself in a loving, halal marriage
  • See your parents' faces full of pride and joy
  • See yourself standing before Allah, and Him being pleased

FEEL that. Then make dua. It will be completely different.

Who You Sit With Changes You

The Prophet ﷺ said: "A person is upon the religion of their close friend, so let one of you look at whom they befriend."

Sunan Abu Dawud 4833, Tirmidhi 2378

The Prophet ﷺ said: "The example of a good companion is like a perfume seller—you will either buy from him, receive a gift, or at least smell something pleasant. The bad companion is like a blacksmith—either he burns your clothes or you leave smelling of smoke."

Sahih al-Bukhari 2101, Sahih Muslim 2628

Friends who normalize removing hijab, gossiping, chasing boys, or comparing bodies will slowly change you—without you even noticing. Make dua for good company. Protect your circle like it's your deen. Because it is.

The Dua for Laylatul Qadr

The Prophet ﷺ taught this dua specifically to Aisha RA for the last 10 nights:

اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي

He chose this dua for a woman. That is not a coincidence. Every sin, every mistake, every moment of weakness—You are forgiven. Now go and dream big.
