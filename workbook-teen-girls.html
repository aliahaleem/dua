Welcome — A Letter to You

You are at one of the most important times of your life. The person you are becoming right now — her habits, her heart, her relationship with Allah — will shape everything that comes after.

This workbook will help you build 7 Dream Duas: specific, vivid, heart-driven supplications that give you butterflies because they are connected to your deepest hopes. Not empty wishes — powerful conversations with the One who can actually answer them.

What is a Dream Dua?
  • It is slightly unrealistic — it excites and slightly scares you
  • It is concrete and specific, not vague
  • It is connected to your Akhira purpose, not just worldly wants
  • It covers multiple areas of life — not just one thing
  • It evolves as you evolve

How to Use This Workbook

  1. Read each section and sit with the ideas before writing
  2. Write honestly — these pages are between you and Allah
  3. Revisit your duas as you grow — they will deepen
  4. Recite them daily, especially in the last third of the night and in sajdah

You Are Not Just a Body

"Indeed, Allah does not look at your appearances or your wealth, but rather He looks at your hearts and your deeds." 📖 Sahih Muslim 2564

In a world that constantly tells you how to look, how to dress, how to perform yourself for other people's approval — this hadith is a lifeline. Allah, the only One whose opinion ultimately matters, is not looking at your body. He is looking at your heart.

That doesn't mean your outer self doesn't matter. It does. But your appearance is a shell — your character and faith are the treasure inside. A shell that cracks and fades. A heart that can keep growing more beautiful forever.

On Hijab & Modesty

وَلْيَضْرِبْنَ بِخُمُرِهِنَّ عَلَىٰ جُيُوبِهِنَّ "And let them draw their head-coverings over their chests…" 📖 Surah An-Nur (24:31)
يُدْنِينَ عَلَيْهِنَّ مِن جَلَابِيبِهِنَّ "O Prophet, tell your wives and daughters and the believing women to draw their outer garments around themselves. That is more suitable that they will be known as Muslim women and not be harmed." 📖 Surah Al-Ahzab (33:59)

Hijab is not just a piece of cloth. It is a declaration: "I am more than what you can see." It is a crown worn by choice — a statement of dignity, identity, and connection to Allah. The woman who wears hijab by conviction is not covering up. She is revealing something far more powerful: her faith.

✍️ What does modesty mean to you beyond clothing — in how you speak, carry yourself, use social media?

Stories That Will Change How You Make Dua

Story 1: The Girl Who Couldn't Afford the Course

A girl desperately wanted to take an Islamic course but had no money. Her mother made dua — specifically, sincerely. Days later, someone returned money they had borrowed years ago, completely given up on. It came at exactly the right time. The daughter took the course.

💡 Allah provides from where you least expect. Ask. Then watch.

وَيَرْزُقْهُ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا يَحْتَسِبُ "And He provides for him from where he does not expect." 📖 Surah At-Talaq (65:3)
Story 2: The Student Who Cried at Night

A student had a major exam tomorrow. She got up in the night, prayed, and cried — genuinely — because the stakes were real. No forced tears. She cried because she cared. That's sincere dua. When your duas are vivid and specific enough, you will naturally pray with that same urgency. Your salah will stop being routine and start being a real conversation.

💡 Dream duas make salah come alive.

Story 3: The Dua Is Higher Than You — Bilal's Lesson

Someone told Bilal his dua wasn't good enough. That advice was wrong. Your dua is not lower than you — it is higher than you. You change to rise to meet your dua. Every time you make it, you become a little more like the person who deserves that answer.

💡 Never shrink your duas. Let them pull you upward.

Story 4: The Woman on the Plane

Shaykh Muhammad al-Shareef was on a flight that seemed about to crash. His wife, even staring death in the face, made dua — not for survival, but for a child. The plane landed safely. She got pregnant. Doctors had said it was impossible. You make dua even when you've been told it can't happen.

💡 Impossible is a word for people who forgot Who they're asking.

Story 5: The Woman Who Had Everything — And Still Should Ask

A man at Hajj sat down and made no dua because he had everything. This is the biggest trap: believing that because life is okay, there is nothing to ask for. There is always your akhira. Your deen. Your legacy. Jannah.

💡 You always have something worth asking Allah for.

How Dream Duas Work

Reactive vs. Proactive Dua

Most people only make dua when things go wrong — that is reactive dua. It is sincere and sweet. But it is not enough. Proactive dua is asking Allah while things are good — out of aspiration, out of dreaming, out of talking to Him as your closest companion even when you don't need rescuing.

Yunus (AS) — swallowed by a whale, in complete darkness — cried out:
"La ilaha illa anta, subhanaka inni kuntu min adh-dhalimeen" 📖 Surah Al-Anbiya (21:87)

That was reactive. Beautiful. But Ibrahim (AS) made proactive dua for righteous children, for a prophet from his descendants, for a city of peace — he asked when life was good, because he was always in conversation with Allah.

✍️ Are your duas mostly reactive (in crisis) or proactive (from aspiration)? What would change if you made proactive duas daily?

Visualize First, Then Ask

Shaykh Muhammad al-Shareef and his wife wanted children for 10 years. His dua had become habit — words without feeling. Then he started imagining his daughter's wedding, his sons leading salah — pictured it in vivid detail. Then he made dua — and it was answered.

The Technique: Before you make dua, close your eyes. Picture the answered version. Where are you? Who is there? What do you feel? Hold that image — then open your hands and ask.

Let my work be sadaqah jariyah that grows even after I am gone.

Like Maryam (AS), let me be someone You chose — not because of my family or my looks, but because of my heart.

3 Identity, Confidence & Beauty

Mentors: Khadija (RA) — confident, respected, never defined by looks. Umm Salamah (RA) — the wise counselor. Khawla bint al-Azwar — brave, dignified, and unapologetically herself.

✍️ My mentor for this category:

Beautiful Inside AND Outside:

Caring for your appearance is halal and good. You can enjoy modest fashion, skincare, fitness, and creativity. But the standard you hold yourself to should come from your values — not from comparison to others. Beauty with depth is far more powerful than beauty alone. Ask Allah to make you beautiful inside and out — and to make your inner beauty the one people remember.

Reflection Questions:

  • What do you love about yourself — truly, not what you perform for others?
  • What insecurities come from comparison? What would change if you stopped comparing?
  • How do you want people to feel after spending time with you?
  • What does "confident in your identity as a Muslim woman" look like for you?
✨ Sample Dream Dua: Ya Allah, make me beautiful in the way that matters most — in my character, my patience, my kindness. Let me love myself the way You made me, without needing anyone's validation. Free me from the trap of comparison. Let me be confident in my identity as a Muslim woman — proud of my hijab, proud of my values, never apologising for who I am. Make my inner light so strong that people feel it when I walk in the room. And let my outer appearance be clean, modest, and beautiful — not to impress, but as a form of gratitude to You.

✍️ What is something you love about how Allah made you that you don't say out loud?

4 Family Relationships

Mentors: Maryam (AS) — supported by Allah alone when her family doubted her. Asiya (RA) — maintained her faith and dignity within a household of oppression.

✍️ My mentor for this category:

Your Mother's Dua:

The dua of a mother for her child is one of the most powerful duas in existence. It goes up with no barrier. Ask your mother to make dua for you — and make dua for her too. Her dua for you is a gift unlike any other.

Reflection Questions:

  • What do you love about your family that you rarely express?
  • What tension or misunderstanding in your family do you want Allah to resolve?
  • How do you want to treat your parents when you are independent and grown?
  • What kind of family environment do you want to help create?
✨ Sample Dream Dua: Ya Allah, make my home a place of genuine peace — not just quiet on the outside, but warm on the inside. Help me be patient with my parents even when I feel misunderstood. Give them the best of health and let me be a cause of their happiness. Resolve what is broken between us. Let us understand each other better with each year. And when I have my own home one day — let it be filled with Your remembrance, laughter, and love.

✍️ What is one thing you wish your family understood about you?

⚠️ The Friends Warning — This is Serious:

"A person follows the religion of his close friend — so look carefully at who you choose." (Abu Dawud 4833)

"A good companion is like a perfume seller — you leave smelling good even if he gave you nothing. A bad companion is like a blacksmith — even if nothing burned you, you go home smelling of smoke." (Bukhari · Muslim)

You don't need to be rude to people who aren't good for you. But you do need to be intentional about who you let close.

✨ Sample Dream Dua: Ya Allah, surround me with friends who remind me of You. Friends who are honest with me, who celebrate my wins without jealousy, and who call me back to my deen gently when I drift. Protect me from friendships built on gossip, comparison, or feeling like I have to be someone I'm not. Give me the courage to be selective. And if good company is hard to find near me — strengthen me to be good even standing alone, like Ibrahim (AS).

7 Health & Mental Wellness

Mentors: Aisha (RA) — active, strong, and emotionally intelligent. Maryam (AS) — patient through great physical and emotional hardship, sustained by Allah alone.

✍️ My mentor for this category:

Reflection Questions:

  • What does your body need that you have been neglecting?
  • What does your mental health need — rest, boundaries, help, community?
  • What would it feel like to be genuinely at peace inside?
  • How can caring for your body be an act of worship?

Remind me You are Al-Ghaffar — when I make mistakes, help me forgive myself. Make my mental health a gift, not a battle. Let me be strong in the way that matters most: rooted in my faith, at peace with my story.
